The Shared Story of Sweden, Norway, and Denmark

From Viking trade routes to modern Nordic cooperation, these three kingdoms have shaped one another for over a thousand years.

Why Their History Is Shared

Sweden, Norway, and Denmark developed in close geographic and political proximity. Seas connected their economies, dynastic marriages linked their rulers, and unions often placed multiple crowns under one ruler. The resulting culture was never identical—but always interwoven.

Today, the countries remain independent constitutional monarchies while collaborating through the Nordic Council, common labor mobility, and shared commitments to social welfare, green transition, and democratic governance.

Denmark

Historically central in the Kalmar Union and maritime power in the Baltic and North Sea.

Sweden

Expanded as a major power in the 17th century and later became a model of modern social democracy.

Norway

Long linked to Denmark and Sweden before full sovereignty in 1905; now known for Arctic strategy and energy wealth.
